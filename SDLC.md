## Software Development Life Cycle Assignment 

## **What is SDLC?**  
    Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is a structured process used by software development teams to plan, design, build, test, deploy, and maintain software applications.

##  **Why is SDLC important?**  
    SDLC helps organizations deliver quality software, reduce project risks, improve project planning, control development costs, meet customer requirements, enhance team collaboration, and ensure proper testing and maintenance.

##  **List the seven phases of SDLC.**
    
    1.  Planning
    2.  Requirements Analysis
    3.  System Design
    4.  Development (Implementation)
    5.  Testing
    6.  Deployment
    7.  Maintenance

##  **Differentiate between functional and non-functional requirements.**
    
      * **Functional Requirements:** Describe what the system should do (e.g., user registration, login, product search).
      * **Non-Functional Requirements:** Describe how the system should perform (e.g., security, performance, scalability, reliability).

##  **What happens during the testing phase?**  
    The testing phase ensures the software works correctly and meets requirements. Its objectives are to find defects, verify functionality, and improve quality through methods such as Unit Testing, Integration Testing, System Testing, and User Acceptance Testing (UAT).

##  **Explain the difference between deployment and maintenance.**
    
      * **Deployment:** The process of releasing software to users, including installing the software, configuring servers, launching the application, and monitoring performance.
      * **Maintenance:** A phase beginning after deployment that continues throughout the software's lifetime to fix bugs, improve performance, add new features, and update security measures.

##  **Compare the Waterfall and Agile models.**
    
      * **Waterfall Model:** A linear approach where each phase must be completed before the next begins; it is simple and well-documented but makes it difficult to accommodate changes, and feedback arrives late.
      * **Agile Model:** Development is divided into small iterations called sprints; it allows for fast delivery, frequent feedback, and flexibility to changing requirements, but it requires active stakeholder involvement and scope may change frequently.

##  **Who are the major stakeholders involved in SDLC?**  
    The major stakeholders are the Project Manager, Business Analyst, UI/UX Designer, Software Developer, Quality Assurance Engineer, System Administrator (DevOps), and End Users.

##  **What is User Acceptance Testing (UAT)?**  
    User Acceptance Testing (UAT) is a testing type conducted by end users to confirm that the system meets business needs or customer aim.

## **Why is maintenance important after deployment?**  
    Maintenance is important after deployment inorder to fix bugs, improve performance, add new features, and update security measures throughout the software's lifetime.
